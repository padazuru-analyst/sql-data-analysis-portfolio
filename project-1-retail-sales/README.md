# Retail Sales Performance Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyses retail sales data to uncover insights about 
revenue performance, customer behaviour, and product trends. 
The analysis was conducted using MySQL and covers 12 transactions 
across 8 customers in Q1 2026.

## Business Questions Answered
1. What is the overall sales performance?
2. Which products and categories drive the most revenue?
3. Who are the most valuable customers?
4. Which city generates the most revenue?
5. How are customers segmented by spending?
6. What does the monthly revenue trend look like?

## Tools Used
- MySQL — data analysis and querying
- GitHub — version control and portfolio hosting

## Database Structure
The database contains two tables:
- **customers** — customer demographics (8 records)
- **sales** — transaction records (12 records)

## Key Findings
- Total revenue: **$8,000** across 12 transactions
- **Electronics dominates** with 78.75% of total revenue
- **Chicago** is the highest revenue city at 40% of total revenue
- **VIP customers** (25% of base) generate 42.5% of total revenue
- **January** was the strongest month at $3,550 in revenue
- **Laptops** are the top performing product at $3,600 total revenue

## SQL Concepts Demonstrated
- SELECT, WHERE, GROUP BY, ORDER BY
- Aggregate functions (SUM, COUNT, AVG, MAX, MIN)
- JOINs and table relationships
- Subqueries and CTEs
- Window functions and RANK()
- CASE statements for segmentation
- Date functions for trend analysis
